How it works?

Typical text classification methodologies rely on big data. This makes sense. Real world practices sometimes require real world data. Ideal distributions don't always represent the world we live in. Many companies cannot compete with major corporations who have massive technical information collection operations in place. This is where Curated News comes in...

We rely on better data. Our open-source headlines classifier, showcases our values and commitment to transparency. The data we used to make this model is freely available for use in accordance with the terms and conditions listed in our codebook. Our dataset was made with the best statistical practices in mind so we could show our competitors that small data doesn't always mean limited use. That said, our dataset contains over 60,000+ observations. It is the largest open-source dataset available when it comes to news-related material.


Example explanations - extreme cases that averaged out...

Example 1
Headline from National Review, The Deeply Flawed Racism Index, originally labelled negative with an overall sentiment score of -1.61
Text classifier score: Neutral 51%, Positive 29%, Negative 19%
The absence of context in this headline makes our text classifier label it neutral. This makes sense. The headline does not actually attempt to elicit an emotional reaction. It might seem volatile subjectively but objectively it does not behave like a clickbait article. It seems to peak a reader's interest naturally without becoming strictly manipulative. This means our model can diffentiate between truly volatile versus subjectively volatile news headlines.

Example 2
Headline from Times of India, Over 60 dead, dozens missing as severe floods strike Europe, originally labelled negative with an overall sentiment score of -1.5
Text classifier score: Negative 100%
There can be no doubt this headline is negative. Its score reflects the immediate context and our text classifier was able to differeniate between the previous headline that had a higher original sentiment score and this one that is clearly not as subjective but which had a lower one. This headline is clickbait material. Even if the article was about floods in all of Europe, using deaths from floods that only represent a fraction of Europe is not representative. If you were European, and wanted to see if these floods affected your specific locality, you would likely feel an urge to click on it because the deaths and lack of specificity enhance your urge to click on it. Volatility is a measure of subjective intentionality from objective statements of intention.
